Wedding planning is one of the most logistically demanding service businesses out there. You're managing dozens of vendors, tracking deposits across 15+ bookings at once, sending contracts at 11pm, and fielding texts from anxious clients on a Sunday morning. The paperwork alone can swallow entire days.

A good CRM doesn't just store contacts — it becomes the operating system for your business. But most CRM tools were built for sales teams, not wedding planners. The ones that work for you are purpose-built for creative service businesses, or at minimum highly customizable.

Here's what actually holds up in 2026.

## Quick Answer

**For most wedding planners, Dubsado or HoneyBook will be the right call.** Both are built specifically for creative service professionals and handle the full client lifecycle: inquiry → proposal → contract → invoice → communication → project management. 

- **Dubsado** is the more powerful and customizable option — better for planners with complex workflows or multiple service tiers.
- **HoneyBook** is cleaner and easier to get started with — better if you want something that works without a lot of setup.
- **17hats** is a strong budget-friendly alternative with solid contract and payment tools.
- **HubSpot** (free tier) works if you need a CRM backbone and don't mind cobbling together contracts and invoicing elsewhere.
- **Pipedrive** is the right pick if you're managing a high volume of leads and want strong pipeline visibility — but it needs integrations for everything else.

---

## What Wedding Planners Actually Need from a CRM

Generic sales CRMs were not designed for the way wedding planners work. Before picking a tool, be honest about your real pain points:

- **Lead capture and inquiry management** — Are inquiries slipping through the cracks? Are you manually copy-pasting info from contact forms into spreadsheets?
- **Proposals and contracts** — Can you send a branded proposal, get it signed, and collect a retainer without switching between 4 different tools?
- **Payment tracking** — Do you know at a glance who owes you money, what's due when, and what's been paid?
- **Client communication** — Can you find the full conversation history in one place, or are you digging through email, texts, and DMs?
- **Timeline and project management** — Can you build a master wedding timeline, assign tasks, and track vendor deliverables?
- **Automation** — Are you manually sending the same follow-up emails over and over?

The tools below are ranked by how well they solve these problems end-to-end.

## The Tools

### 1. Dubsado — Best for Power Users

[Dubsado](https://dubsado.com/?ref=fullstaxx) is the most feature-complete CRM for wedding planners who want full control. It handles the entire client lifecycle with deep workflow automation: auto-send inquiry responses, proposals, contracts, invoices, and follow-ups based on triggers you define.

**What makes it work for wedding planners:**
- **Canned emails + workflows** — build a complete onboarding sequence that fires automatically when a client books
- **Smart contracts** — embedded forms collect client info, signatures, and payments in a single branded flow
- **Payment plans** — set up installment schedules tied to specific dates (deposit, 6 months out, 30 days out, final balance)
- **Client portals** — couples get a dedicated portal to view their contract, make payments, fill out forms, and message you
- **Form builder** — build questionnaires for venue preferences, vendor wishes, day-of timelines

**What to know:** Dubsado has a real learning curve. The setup takes time — probably a full weekend to configure properly. But once it's dialed in, it runs almost automatically. Their free trial lets you onboard up to 3 clients before paying.

**Pricing:** ~$20/month (monthly) or ~$200/year

---

### 2. HoneyBook — Best for Getting Started Fast

[HoneyBook](https://honeybook.com/?ref=fullstaxx) is the most popular CRM in the wedding industry for good reason: it's purpose-built, beautifully designed, and genuinely easy to use. It hits all the core needs without overwhelming you with configuration options.

**What makes it work for wedding planners:**
- **All-in-one client files** — every inquiry, email, contract, invoice, and payment lives in one project file
- **Smart files** — combine proposals, contracts, and invoices into a single send that clients can sign and pay in one flow
- **Automations** — trigger emails, reminders, and tasks automatically after key client actions
- **Pipeline view** — see all your leads and active clients in a kanban board by stage
- **Mobile app** — actually good, unlike most CRM mobile apps

**What to know:** HoneyBook is less customizable than Dubsado. If you have complex multi-service packages or need granular workflow control, you'll hit its ceiling. But for most solo planners or small teams, it's all you need.

**Pricing:** ~$19/month (essential) or ~$39/month (premium)

---

### 3. 17hats — Best Budget Option

[17hats](https://17hats.com/?ref=fullstaxx) has been around since 2013 and is the most underrated CRM in this space. It's not as flashy as HoneyBook or as powerful as Dubsado, but it covers contracts, invoicing, questionnaires, and scheduling — and it's cheaper.

**What makes it work for wedding planners:**
- **Solid contract + invoice tools** — templates are easy to set up, legally structured, and send cleanly
- **Lead capture forms** — embed on your website to pull inquiries directly into your CRM
- **Bookkeeping** — basic income/expense tracking built in, no QuickBooks needed for simple financials
- **Time tracking** — useful for billing by the hour or tracking time per event

**What to know:** The UI feels dated compared to HoneyBook. The automation tools are functional but less intuitive. If aesthetics and workflow polish matter to you, you'll probably outgrow it. But if you just want something that works without paying HoneyBook prices, 17hats delivers.

**Pricing:** ~$45/month (but frequently runs steep discounts for annual plans — worth checking)

---

### 4. HubSpot — Best Free CRM Backbone

[HubSpot's free CRM](https://hubspot.com/?ref=fullstaxx) is genuinely powerful and genuinely free for the core features. It's not purpose-built for wedding planners, but if you already have tools for contracts and invoicing (or want to use something like DocuSign + Stripe separately), HubSpot gives you a very capable contact and pipeline management layer.

**What makes it work for wedding planners:**
- **Free forever** for core CRM features — contact records, deal pipeline, email tracking, notes
- **Email sequences** — automate follow-up emails to inquiries (paid feature, but basic sequences are free)
- **Deal pipeline** — customize stages to match your booking funnel
- **Gmail/Outlook integration** — all emails sync automatically to client records
- **Reporting** — better analytics than most purpose-built tools

**What to know:** HubSpot does not handle contracts, invoices, or payment collection natively. You'll need separate tools for those — which adds friction and monthly cost. It's best for planners who already have their contract/payment workflow handled and just need a proper CRM to manage leads and communication.

**Pricing:** Free (core CRM) — paid plans start at $20/month/seat

---

### 5. Pipedrive — Best for Lead-Heavy Operations

[Pipedrive](https://pipedrive.com/?ref=fullstaxx) is a sales-focused CRM with the best pipeline management in this list. If you're doing high-volume marketing, attending bridal expos, or generating a lot of leads that need systematic follow-up, Pipedrive's visual pipeline and automation tools are hard to beat.

**What makes it work for wedding planners:**
- **Visual pipeline** — drag-and-drop deal stages, clear overview of your entire pipeline
- **Activity reminders** — never miss a follow-up call or email
- **Email integration** — two-way email sync, templates, tracking
- **Automations** — trigger follow-up tasks automatically when deals hit certain stages
- **Integrations** — connects to Zapier, DocuSign, Stripe, and hundreds of others

**What to know:** Like HubSpot, Pipedrive doesn't handle contracts, invoices, or payments natively. You're building a stack, not getting an all-in-one. For most solo wedding planners, Dubsado or HoneyBook will be more practical. Pipedrive makes sense if you're running a larger operation with a team or a high volume of inquiries that need a proper sales process.

**Pricing:** ~$14/month (Essential) — most useful features require ~$29/month (Advanced)

## How to Actually Choose

**You're just getting started and want something simple:** Go with HoneyBook. It's the fastest path from "I have no system" to "I have a real client onboarding flow." Most wedding planners who use it never leave.

**You want maximum control and automation:** Go with Dubsado. Budget a weekend to set it up properly — the workflows, canned emails, and automated payment schedules are worth it once they're built. You'll reclaim hours every week.

**You're watching your budget tightly:** Try 17hats. It's not the prettiest, but it handles the fundamentals — contracts, invoices, questionnaires — and it's cheaper than the competition.

**You already have contract/payment tools and just need a CRM:** HubSpot free tier is hard to argue with. Pair it with DocuSign (or HelloSign) and Stripe/PayPal and you have a solid stack for close to nothing.

**You're running a larger team or generating a lot of leads from marketing:** Look at Pipedrive. The pipeline management and team features are the best in class, and it integrates with whatever contract/payment tools you already use.

## Bottom Line

For most wedding planners — especially solo operators and small teams — **HoneyBook or Dubsado will be the right choice**. Both are purpose-built, both handle the full client workflow, and both have strong communities of wedding pros who can help you get set up.

If you're still on spreadsheets and Gmail folders, either of those tools will change your business in the first month. The time you spend on admin will drop significantly. Clients will have a more professional experience. You'll actually know where your money is.

Pick one, commit to setting it up properly, and stop letting contracts and invoices sit in a pile.
