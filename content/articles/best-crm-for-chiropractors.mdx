Running a chiropractic practice means juggling patient intake, appointment scheduling, SOAP notes, insurance billing, and follow-up reminders — all at once, often with a skeleton staff. Generic CRMs like Salesforce were built for sales pipelines, not patient care. The wrong tool creates busywork instead of eliminating it.

We tested the most popular options used by chiropractic practices, evaluated them against real workflows, and came up with concrete picks. Here's what you need to know.

## Quick Answer

**For most chiropractic practices, Jane App is the best CRM.** It handles scheduling, intake forms, charting, online booking, and patient communication in one HIPAA-compliant platform. If you want the most robust chiro-specific clinical tools, ChiroTouch is the deeper option — but it costs more and takes longer to learn. If budget is the primary constraint, Zoho CRM can work as a lightweight CRM layer on top of a separate scheduler.

---

## What Chiropractors Actually Need from a CRM

Before diving into specific tools, it's worth being precise about the problem. Chiropractors don't need a "sales CRM" — they need a **patient relationship management** system that handles:

- **Online booking and self-scheduling** — patients want to book themselves
- **Automated appointment reminders** — SMS and email to reduce no-shows
- **Digital intake forms** — collect health history before the first visit
- **Patient communication history** — who called, when, and why
- **Follow-up sequences** — reactivate lapsed patients automatically
- **HIPAA compliance** — non-negotiable if you're storing any health data
- **Billing and insurance notes** (or at least integration with billing software)

Generic CRMs cover some of this. Purpose-built chiropractic software covers most of it. The right answer depends on how complex your practice is.

### 2. ChiroTouch — Best for Clinical Depth

ChiroTouch has been around for over two decades and is purpose-built for chiropractic. If Jane is the polished modern option, ChiroTouch is the deep specialist tool — more clinical features, more configuration, and a steeper setup process.

**What stands out:**

ChiroTouch's documentation tools are more chiropractic-specific than most competitors. It includes outcome assessments, body diagrams, and pre-built chiro SOAP templates that align closely with how chiropractors actually document. This saves time on charting if you're doing high volume.

The patient flow management is also strong — front desk check-in kiosks, room tracking, and provider queues all work together, which matters if you're seeing 30+ patients per day.

ChiroTouch also has a robust billing module and clearinghouse integrations, which is meaningful if you handle a lot of insurance.

**Limitations:** It's more expensive than Jane (pricing typically starts around $200+/month), and onboarding takes real effort. It's also a desktop-first application — the mobile experience is less seamless.

**Best for:** High-volume practices with complex insurance billing needs, or practitioners who want deeply chiro-specific documentation tools.

### 3. Zoho CRM — Best Budget Option

Zoho CRM isn't built for healthcare, but it's flexible enough that cash-pay-only or cash-heavy practices can make it work — especially if they're already using a separate scheduling tool like Acuity or Jane for bookings.

**What stands out:**

Zoho CRM is genuinely powerful for the price. Contact management, follow-up sequences, email/SMS automation, and pipeline tracking are all solid. If you treat "patient relationships" the way a service business tracks client relationships, it maps reasonably well.

You can build out automated follow-up workflows — for example, if a patient hasn't booked in 60 days, trigger an email sequence. That kind of reactivation marketing is actually where Zoho shines compared to practice management software.

**Limitations:** No HIPAA BAA on the standard plan (you'd need Zoho's enterprise tiers and a custom agreement). No built-in charting or intake forms. You're stitching together a stack rather than using one platform.

**Best for:** Cash-pay practices on a tight budget, or practices that want a dedicated CRM/marketing layer and are fine managing appointments in a separate system.

## What About EHR Platforms Like Kareo or SimplePractice?

You'll see Kareo (now Tebra) and SimplePractice come up in searches for chiropractic software. Both are worth knowing about:

- **Tebra (Kareo)** is a full EHR + practice management + billing platform aimed at medical practices generally. It's comprehensive but designed for a broader clinical audience, and pricing reflects that.
- **SimplePractice** is excellent but oriented toward mental health and therapy practices. Charting templates don't map well to chiropractic workflows, and it lacks chiro-specific body diagram tools.

For most chiropractors, neither is the right default pick unless you have specific billing complexity or are part of a multi-specialty clinic.

## How to Choose

**Go with Jane App if:** You want one platform that handles scheduling, intake, charting, reminders, and patient communication without a long implementation process. It works well for solo practitioners and practices up to 5-6 providers.

**Go with ChiroTouch if:** You're running a high-volume practice, handle significant insurance volume, and want clinical documentation tools built specifically for chiropractic care. Budget for onboarding time.

**Go with Zoho CRM if:** You're cash-pay only, already have a scheduling system, and want powerful patient follow-up and reactivation marketing without paying practice management software prices.

## The Bottom Line

The "best CRM for chiropractors" is really a question of what kind of practice you run. For the majority of chiropractors — especially those just getting started or running a solo/small group practice — **Jane App** hits the sweet spot of features, usability, and price.

If your practice does high volume or heavy insurance billing, **ChiroTouch** is worth the investment and the learning curve. And if you're bootstrapping and need to keep costs low, **Zoho CRM** paired with a free or low-cost scheduler can get the job done until you're ready to upgrade.

The worst outcome is sticking with spreadsheets and sticky notes because picking software feels overwhelming. Any of these three options will meaningfully improve how your practice runs.
