If you've been running a wedding planning business for more than six months, you've almost certainly heard someone argue passionately about Dubsado vs HoneyBook. In Facebook groups, at conferences, on every wedding industry podcast — the debate never ends. That's not a coincidence. These two platforms dominate the CRM space for wedding creatives, and they're genuinely different products aimed at subtly different people.

The short version: HoneyBook is faster to set up and easier to maintain, but Dubsado gives you more control over every detail. Neither is universally better. The right pick depends on how you run your business — and we're going to help you figure that out.

## Quick Answer

**Choose HoneyBook if** you want an all-in-one platform that's fast to learn, has clean client-facing experiences, and you don't want to spend hours configuring automations.

**Choose Dubsado if** you need fine-grained control over your workflows, want deeply customizable proposals and contracts, and don't mind a steeper setup curve.

---

## What Both Platforms Do

Before getting into the differences, it's worth acknowledging the overlap. Both Dubsado and HoneyBook handle the core workflow every wedding planner needs:

- **Client intake:** inquiry forms, contact management
- **Proposals & contracts:** send, sign, and track electronically
- **Invoicing & payments:** collect deposits, schedule payment plans
- **Client portals:** a dedicated login for clients to access their documents
- **Automations:** trigger emails, tasks, and actions based on project status
- **Scheduling:** appointment booking integrated with your calendar

That overlap is exactly why the comparison is so hard. You need to look at *how* each platform handles these features — not just whether it has them.

## Pricing: Closer Than You Think

Both platforms have landed at nearly identical price points, which makes the decision almost entirely about features and fit rather than cost.

**Dubsado** charges $20/month for the Starter plan (limited to 3 active projects — not useful for most planners) and $40/month for Premier, which removes all limits and unlocks public proposals, payment plans, and advanced scheduling. There's an annual option that drops Premier to $33/month.

**HoneyBook** runs $19/month for Essentials and $39/month for Growth. The Growth plan adds priority support, multiple users, and a few advanced automation features. The annual pricing lands at $16 and $32 respectively.

Neither charges a percentage of your invoices (beyond payment processing fees), which puts them both ahead of platforms like 17hats or older competitors that take a cut of revenue.

## Workflows and Automation: Dubsado's Biggest Advantage

This is where Dubsado pulls ahead for planners who live and die by their systems.

Dubsado's workflow builder lets you chain together dozens of actions: send an email, wait 3 days, check if a form was submitted, send a follow-up if it wasn't, create a task, apply a package, trigger another workflow. It's genuinely powerful — and genuinely complex. Setting up a complete wedding workflow in Dubsado takes most users several weekends and a lot of trial and error.

HoneyBook's automations are simpler. You pick a trigger (inquiry received, contract signed, payment made) and attach an action or a sequence. It covers 80% of what most planners need, but you can't build the kind of deeply conditional logic Dubsado supports. For planners who want to automate every possible touchpoint with minimal manual intervention, Dubsado wins.

For planners who want "set it up in an afternoon and move on," HoneyBook wins.

## Contracts and Proposals: A Real Difference in Philosophy

Wedding planning contracts are serious documents. Both platforms let you build contract templates, add signature blocks, and collect legally binding electronic signatures — but the editing experience is very different.

**Dubsado's contract editor** is essentially a rich text editor with merge fields (called "smart fields"). You write your contract from scratch or paste one in, add variable fields like `{client_name}` and `{event_date}`, and the platform populates them when you send. It's powerful, but it requires you to actually write your contract.

**HoneyBook's contract experience** is more opinionated. It offers pre-built templates written by actual attorneys, and the editor is more structured. The finished product looks polished with less effort, but you have less flexibility over the precise formatting and conditional clauses.

For planners with existing contracts who just need to digitize them: Dubsado. For planners starting from scratch who want something that looks professional without hiring a lawyer first: HoneyBook's templates are genuinely good.

## Client Portal: HoneyBook's Edge

This one isn't close. HoneyBook's client experience is significantly more polished in 2026. The client portal looks like a modern SaaS product — clean, mobile-friendly, and intuitive for clients who've never used a wedding planning portal before.

Dubsado's client portal is functional but feels dated. Clients can log in, view proposals, sign contracts, and make payments — but the UI hasn't kept pace with HoneyBook. If your brand is built around a premium, white-glove experience, this matters.

That said, Dubsado does allow more branding customization. You can set custom colors, fonts, and logos throughout the entire client-facing experience. HoneyBook has improved its branding options but still lags slightly on fine-grained customization.

## Mobile App: HoneyBook by a Mile

If you're managing clients from your phone — which most active wedding planners do — this gap matters more than people realize.

HoneyBook's mobile app is full-featured. You can send proposals, collect signatures, process payments, and respond to inquiries without touching a desktop. The app gets regular updates and is consistently well-reviewed.

Dubsado's mobile app is, to be blunt, not good. It exists, you can check project status and send messages, but you can't do meaningful workflow management from it. If you're at a venue walkthrough and a client needs a contract, you're pulling out a laptop.

## Payment Processing

Both platforms handle invoicing and deposits, but the payment infrastructure differs.

HoneyBook has a built-in payment processor. The fee is 2.9% + 25¢ per transaction — standard Stripe/card rates. The advantage is everything is native; payments, reconciliation, and client records all live in one place.

Dubsado integrates with Stripe and PayPal, which gives you more flexibility (especially if you already have Stripe set up with preferred rates). The tradeoff is a bit more setup.

For planners who take ACH bank transfers (common for large wedding deposits), both platforms support it, but HoneyBook has made the ACH experience more seamless.
