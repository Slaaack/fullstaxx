Most coaches don't struggle with the coaching itself — they struggle with everything around it. Onboarding new clients, sending contracts, following up on unpaid invoices, remembering where each client is in their program, scheduling discovery calls, and keeping notes from 12 different sessions organized. That's the work that eats your time and doesn't show up on your website.

A good CRM fixes most of this. A bad one adds another tool to manage. We reviewed the platforms coaches are actually using — from solo life coaches to multi-coach businesses — and here's what holds up.

## What Coaches Actually Need in a CRM

General-purpose CRMs like Salesforce or HubSpot are built for sales teams. Coaches have different needs:

- **Client onboarding automation** — send a contract, collect a signature, take payment, and schedule the first call without touching it manually
- **Session scheduling** — calendar integration, booking links, buffer times, timezone handling
- **Contract and proposal management** — coaching agreements, scope of work, digital signatures
- **Invoicing and payment** — one-time, recurring, or package-based payments
- **Session notes and client history** — a central record of what was discussed, what was committed to, what's outstanding
- **Client accountability tools** — goal tracking, homework, check-ins (not all coaches need this, but many want it)
- **Pipeline visibility** — how many prospects are in discovery, how many are active clients, who's up for renewal
- **Communication history** — all emails and messages in one place per client, not scattered across your inbox

### 2. Dubsado — Best for Workflow Automation Power Users

Dubsado and HoneyBook compete directly, and the choice often comes down to one thing: how much control do you want over your workflows? Dubsado gives you more — more conditional logic, more trigger options, more flexibility in how automations chain together. The tradeoff is a steeper learning curve and a less polished interface.

**What makes it stand out:**

- Workflow builder with conditional branching — if a client does X, do Y; if they don't respond in 48 hours, send a follow-up. More logic than HoneyBook's automations.
- Unlimited clients and projects on all plans — no surprise scaling costs
- Custom forms that are genuinely powerful — intake questionnaires, feedback forms, session prep forms, all automatable
- Lead capture forms that plug directly into your website and trigger a workflow automatically
- Strong community — the Dubsado Facebook group is very active, and there's a large ecosystem of setup specialists and template creators

If you have a well-defined coaching process with multiple touchpoints, Dubsado's automation depth pays off. If you're just getting started and want something that works out of the box, HoneyBook is faster to implement.

**Pricing:** $20/month (Starter, limited to 3 clients), $40/month (Premier, unlimited). Free trial available (limited to 3 clients).

**Downside:** No native scheduling tool — you'll need Calendly or a similar integration. Interface feels dated compared to HoneyBook. The free trial limitation (3 clients) makes it hard to fully evaluate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between HoneyBook and Dubsado for coaches?

Both handle contracts, invoices, forms, and client communication. The key differences: HoneyBook has better UX, built-in scheduling, and is faster to set up. Dubsado has more powerful workflow automation with conditional logic and is more flexible for complex processes. HoneyBook is better for most coaches; Dubsado is better for coaches who have a highly structured process and want to automate every touchpoint.

### Do coaches need a CRM or can they use a spreadsheet?

A spreadsheet works when you have fewer than 5 active clients. Beyond that, manual tracking becomes a liability — missed follow-ups, lost contract versions, forgotten invoices. A CRM automates the administrative layer so you can focus on the coaching. Most coaches who switch to a CRM say they wish they'd done it sooner.

### Is there a free CRM for coaches?

HubSpot's free CRM is the most capable free option, but it's a sales CRM — not built for coaching delivery. Dubsado offers a free trial limited to 3 clients. HoneyBook and CoachAccountable both have trial periods. For a truly free ongoing solution, HubSpot + Google Calendar + a free contract tool like DocuSign's free tier is a functional stack for early-stage coaches.

### Can I use a CRM to run group coaching programs?

CoachAccountable is the best option for group coaching — it has built-in group workspaces, shared milestones, group check-ins, and the ability to manage multiple clients through the same program simultaneously. Kajabi and Teachable are worth considering if courses and community are central to your group program model.

### What do coaches use for contracts and e-signatures?

HoneyBook and Dubsado both have contract templates with digital signatures built in. If you want a standalone contract tool, DocuSign (free tier available) and PandaDoc are widely used. Most coaches find it cleaner to have contracts inside their CRM rather than in a separate tool.

### How much should a coach spend on a CRM?

Most independent coaches land in the $20–$40/month range and get everything they need. HoneyBook Essentials at $39/month is the most common choice for established coaches. If you're just starting out and under 3 clients, Dubsado's free trial or HubSpot's free tier works fine. Spending more than $100/month on a CRM is rarely justified unless you're running a multi-coach organization.
