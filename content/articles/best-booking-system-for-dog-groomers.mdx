## What Dog Groomers Actually Need in Booking Software

Generic scheduling tools like Calendly or Square Appointments are fine for haircuts and yoga classes. Dog grooming is different. Here's what to look for:

- **Pet profiles** — store breed, size, temperament, coat type, and health notes
- **Breed-based pricing** — a Great Dane costs more to groom than a Chihuahua; the software should handle this automatically
- **Appointment type complexity** — bath + brush, full groom, nail trim, de-shedding, etc.
- **No-show protection** — deposits, credit card holds, or automated reminders
- **Multi-pet bookings** — clients often bring more than one dog
- **Mobile-friendly** — groomers work on their feet, not at a desk

## What to Watch Out For

**No-show fees matter more in grooming than you think.** A client who doesn't show for a 90-minute grooming appointment is expensive — that slot is gone. Look for systems that let you require a card on file or collect a deposit at booking. MoeGo, Vagaro, and Square all support this.

**Automated reminders dramatically reduce no-shows.** In our conversations with groomers, the tools that send reminders 48 hours and 24 hours before appointments see 30-50% fewer no-shows than those that don't. This alone justifies a paid plan.

**Don't underestimate pet notes.** A dog's first visit is fine. The fifth visit — when you know she's nervous around the blow dryer and her owner wants a specific ear trim — is where pet history earns its keep. Systems without pet profiles force you to keep your own notes somewhere else, which inevitably get lost.
