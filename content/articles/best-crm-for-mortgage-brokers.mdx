## What Makes a Good Mortgage CRM?

Before getting into the picks, here's what actually matters in this category:

- **LOS Integration** — Can it sync with Encompass, BytePro, Calyx, or your loan origination system of choice?
- **Compliance-Aware Automation** — RESPA and TRID require careful handling of referral partner communication. Your CRM should help, not expose you.
- **Lead-to-Close Pipeline** — Stages need to match how mortgages actually work: inquiry → pre-qual → application → processing → underwriting → closing → post-close.
- **Referral Partner Management** — Realtors, financial advisors, and attorneys are your lifeblood. Your CRM needs to nurture them too, not just borrowers.
- **Multi-Channel Outreach** — Email, text, and voicemail drops should all be native or tightly integrated.

## What to Look for If You're Shopping Now

**Integration with your LOS first.** Everything else is secondary. If your CRM can't sync loan status from Encompass (or whichever LOS you're on), you're double-entering data and your pipeline will be inaccurate within a week.

**Compliance guardrails matter more than you think.** Automated marketing to referral partners has RESPA landmines. Look for CRMs that include guidance or built-in restrictions on co-marketing content. The last thing you want is your automation system creating a compliance issue.

**Think about your contact mix.** If 60% of your business is purchase referrals from realtors, lean toward a CRM with strong partner management (Whiteboard, Total Expert). If you run a heavy refi or rate-watch business, Total Expert's credit monitoring and rate-trigger features are worth the premium.

**Ask about migration.** If you have years of borrower data in another system, the real cost of switching is data migration and re-engagement of dormant contacts. Most vendors offer migration assistance — ask how thorough it is before you commit.
