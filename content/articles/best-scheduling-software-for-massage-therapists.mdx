## What Massage Therapists Actually Need in Scheduling Software

A generic booking tool like Calendly works fine for consultants. Massage therapy is different. Here's what matters:

- **Online booking with service selection** — clients should be able to book a 60-min deep tissue vs. 90-min prenatal without calling you
- **Client intake forms** — health history, contraindications, pressure preferences. Ideally digital and auto-attached to the appointment
- **No-show protection** — deposits, credit card holds, cancellation policies. No-shows are a real revenue problem for bodyworkers
- **Packages and memberships** — selling 5-session bundles or monthly memberships is a major income lever; your software needs to track redemptions
- **Gift certificates** — a significant revenue source, especially around holidays
- **HIPAA considerations** — massage therapists handle health information. Not all are legally required to be fully HIPAA-compliant, but many clients expect it and some insurance billing workflows require it
- **SOAP note storage** — clinical therapists document every session; having notes attached to client records is essential
- **Automated reminders** — SMS and email reminders reduce no-shows by 30–50% in most practices
- **Mobile-friendly** — you're often working between sessions, not at a desk

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does scheduling software need to be HIPAA compliant for massage therapists?

It depends. Massage therapists who are not billing insurance and don't share health information with other providers are often not classified as "covered entities" under HIPAA — meaning strict compliance isn't legally required. However, if you store detailed health histories, work with insurance, or operate in a clinical setting, HIPAA compliance is worth taking seriously. Jane App is the only option in this roundup that's fully HIPAA compliant out of the box. Vagaro and MindBody offer HIPAA-compliant plans as add-ons.

### What's the best free scheduling software for massage therapists?

Square Appointments is the best free option — it handles online booking, automated reminders, and payment processing at no monthly cost for solo practitioners. The tradeoff is no SOAP notes, no memberships, and no consumer marketplace. It's a solid starting point but most growing practices outgrow it within a year.

### How do I reduce no-shows as a massage therapist?

The most effective tactics: (1) require a credit card to hold the appointment even if you don't charge upfront, (2) send automated reminders at 48 hours and again 2–4 hours before the appointment, (3) set a clear cancellation policy (24 or 48 hours) and enforce it consistently. All five platforms in this roundup support automated reminders. MindBody, Vagaro, and Jane App all support credit card holds and configurable cancellation policies.

### Can I sell gift certificates through scheduling software?

Yes — MindBody, Vagaro, Jane App, Square Appointments, and Booksy all support gift certificates. Vagaro and MindBody make it particularly easy: you get a shareable purchase link you can post on your website or social media, and redemptions are tracked automatically against your client records. Gift certificate sales spike around Mother's Day, Valentine's Day, and the holidays — worth having set up before those windows.

### What's the difference between MindBody and Vagaro for massage therapy?

MindBody is built for scale — multi-location studios, large teams, complex membership programs, and serious marketing infrastructure. It's more powerful but significantly more expensive ($129–$349/month vs. Vagaro's $30/month) and has a steeper learning curve. Vagaro hits the sweet spot for most independent therapists and small practices: nearly as many features, a fraction of the cost, easier to set up and manage. Unless you're running a real studio operation, Vagaro is the better starting point.

### Do I need scheduling software with SOAP note storage?

If you're practicing clinically — documenting conditions, tracking treatment progress, or working with referred clients — yes, absolutely. SOAP note storage protects you legally, improves client outcomes, and is expected in any professional clinical context. Jane App has the best SOAP note system in this category. Vagaro's built-in SOAP notes are solid for most use cases. MindBody offers SOAP notes as an add-on. Square and Booksy don't have them at all.
