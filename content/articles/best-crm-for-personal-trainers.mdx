## What Personal Trainers Actually Need From a CRM

Before we get into the rankings, it's worth being specific about what "CRM" means in the context of personal training — because it's a little different from, say, a sales CRM at a software company.

For personal trainers, a CRM needs to:

- Store and display client health history, goals, and notes in one place
- Handle session scheduling without double-booking
- Send automated reminders so clients don't ghost their 6 AM slot
- Process recurring billing or package payments
- Track progress metrics (weight, reps, measurements) over time
- Optionally: deliver workout programs and nutrition plans

Most generic CRMs (HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho) do the contact management part fine but fall flat on the fitness-specific stuff. That's why we included a mix of fitness-native platforms and adaptable general tools.

## The Top CRM Options for Personal Trainers

### 1. Trainerize — Best Overall for Independent Trainers

Trainerize was built specifically for fitness coaches, and it shows. The client-facing app is polished, and clients actually use it — which is more than you can say for most platforms that claim to have a "client portal."

From the trainer side, you get a full view of each client: their program history, habit streaks, body measurements, check-in notes, and messaging history. Sessions can be booked directly through the app, and automated check-in reminders go out without you doing anything.

The business side is solid too. Packages, subscriptions, and one-time payments all run through Stripe integration, and you can set up automated billing so you're not chasing invoices every month.

Where Trainerize falls short: it's not great for trainers managing a full studio with walk-in class schedules. It's optimized for 1:1 and small-group online coaching, not front-desk gym management.

**Pricing:** Starts at $5/month for 2 clients; scales to $70/month for unlimited clients with full business features.

### 2. Mindbody — Best for Studio Owners and Multi-Trainer Teams

Mindbody is the industry standard for gyms and fitness studios, and there's a reason it's been around for 20+ years. If you're managing a facility with multiple trainers, group classes, retail sales, and membership plans, Mindbody can handle all of it in one system.

The scheduling engine is genuinely impressive — it handles class capacity, waitlists, trainer-specific availability, and integrates with your website for self-booking. The point-of-sale system is robust enough for studios selling supplements and merchandise alongside memberships.

That said, Mindbody is significantly more complex than solo trainers need. Setup takes real time, the interface hasn't aged as gracefully as competitors, and pricing starts at $129/month — which is hard to justify if you have 15 clients and train out of a commercial gym.

It's the right choice if you're running (or planning to run) a small studio. It's overkill if you're a solo trainer working with clients 1:1.

**Pricing:** Starts at $129/month. Enterprise plans available.

### 3. HoneyBook — Best for High-Ticket, Premium Training Clients

HoneyBook isn't a fitness platform — it's a CRM built for service-based freelancers (photographers, event planners, consultants). But if your personal training business looks more like a premium coaching practice than a gym, it's surprisingly good fit.

Here's what HoneyBook does exceptionally well: the client onboarding workflow. You can send a lead a single link that walks them through an intake questionnaire, a service agreement, and an invoice — all in one flow. For trainers charging $800+/month for nutrition + coaching packages, this kind of professionalism matters.

The automated follow-up sequences are also well-designed. If a prospect fills out your intake form but doesn't book, HoneyBook can send timed follow-up emails automatically — something most fitness-native tools can't do.

The tradeoffs: no fitness-specific features at all. No workout program builder, no habit tracking, no body composition logging. If you need those, you'd be pairing HoneyBook with an app like Trainerize, which adds cost and complexity.

**Pricing:** $19/month for Starter, $39/month for Essentials (most trainers would need Essentials for automations).

### 4. PT Distinction — Best for Online Coaches Delivering Structured Programs

PT Distinction occupies a similar niche to Trainerize but leans harder into the programming and content delivery side. If you sell structured 12-week programs or have clients following detailed periodized training blocks, PT Distinction's exercise library (10,000+ exercises with video) and program builder are genuinely best-in-class.

Client management basics are covered: messaging, automated check-ins, goal tracking. Billing runs through Stripe. The interface is functional if not particularly modern.

Where it lags: the scheduling and calendar features are limited compared to Trainerize, and it doesn't have the same polished client-facing app experience. It's a coaching tool first, CRM second.

**Pricing:** $19/month for up to 5 clients; $69/month for unlimited.

### 5. Practice Better — Best for Trainers Who Also Do Nutrition Coaching

If your practice combines fitness with nutrition counseling or health coaching, Practice Better is worth a look. It was designed for dietitians and health coaches, so it handles food journals, supplement tracking, and session notes in a way fitness-only platforms don't.

The CRM side is clean — client profiles, messaging, document storage, session scheduling. The intake form and package management features are solid. It's not as flashy as Trainerize on the fitness programming side, but if you're billing for a holistic "body composition coaching" service, it might fit better.

**Pricing:** Starts at $25/month; $60/month for the professional plan with automations.

## What to Look For When Choosing

**Your business model matters most.** A trainer doing in-person 1:1 sessions at a commercial gym has different needs than someone running an online coaching business with 80 remote clients. Trainerize is built for the latter; Mindbody skews toward the former.

**Client experience is underrated.** The best CRM is the one your clients actually use. Platforms with poor mobile apps result in clients ignoring automated check-ins and not logging their workouts — which undermines the whole point.

**Start simple.** Most trainers starting out don't need Mindbody's complexity. A $19/month Trainerize or PT Distinction plan gets you 90% of the way there and won't paralyze you with features you'll never use.

**Check the payment processing fees.** Most platforms use Stripe and charge 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction on top of their monthly fee. That adds up. Mindbody has its own payment processing at competitive rates if you're doing high volume.

## The Verdict

For most independent personal trainers, **Trainerize is the clearest recommendation**. It's the rare tool that's both genuinely useful for coaches and actually adopted by clients — which is the whole point. The free trial is generous enough to test it with real clients before committing.

If you're growing toward a studio model, budget for **Mindbody** from the start rather than migrating later — the setup cost is real, but so is the pain of switching platforms with 200 active clients.

And if you're running a premium coaching practice where the sales and onboarding process matters as much as the training itself, **HoneyBook** paired with a lightweight program app is a genuinely underrated combination.
