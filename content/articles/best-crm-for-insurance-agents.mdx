## What Makes a CRM Good for Insurance?

Most CRM reviews test generic sales workflows. Insurance is different. Here's what we weighted heavily:

- **Policy and renewal tracking** — Can you log coverage types, premium amounts, and renewal dates without a dozen custom fields?
- **Automated follow-up sequences** — Renewal reminders, birthday emails, cross-sell nudges. Set once, run forever.
- **Lead source attribution** — Knowing whether leads came from referrals, Google, or direct mail matters when you're deciding where to spend.
- **Compliance-friendly** — HIPAA-adjacent considerations for health agents; state-specific note-keeping for others.
- **Integration with raters and quoting tools** — Bonus points for EZLynx, Turborater, or AMS360 integration.

## What About AMS vs. CRM?

A common question: do you need an AMS (Agency Management System) like Applied Epic or AMS360, or a CRM?

**Short answer:** They solve different problems.

- **AMS** is your system of record for policies, endorsements, certificates, and billing — it's a compliance and operations tool.
- **CRM** is your system of record for *relationships* — leads, pipeline, follow-up, and client communication.

Many agents use both. Your AMS tracks the policy; your CRM tracks the relationship around it. AgencyZoom and several others are designed to work alongside an AMS, not replace it. If you're a solo agent writing a handful of policies per month, you can probably get away with CRM-only. If you're running an established agency, you likely need both.
